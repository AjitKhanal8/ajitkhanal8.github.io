Working Papers

Distributional Assessment of the Impact of Imports and Exports on Net Farm Income

with Le Wang

This research examines how international trade affects the distribution of farm income across different producer segments and regions using advanced distributional analysis techniques.

Evaluating North American Dairy Trade Pre- and Post-USMCA Using a Quantitative Trade Model

with Jason H. Grant and Nicolas Legrand

Using quantitative trade models to assess the impacts of the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement on dairy trade flows and market outcomes across North America.

Impact of Agricultural Imports on Farmland Rental Rates: A Causal ML Approach

with Le Wang

Employing causal machine learning methods to identify heterogeneous treatment effects of agricultural imports on farmland rental rates across different regions and farm characteristics.

Impact of Edge of Field Practices on Crop Productivity: An Event Study Approach

with Nicolas Legrand and Jason H. Grant

Using event study methodology to evaluate the causal impact of conservation practices on agricultural productivity and environmental outcomes.

Impact of Consolidation of Meat Packing Industry on Producer and Consumer Welfare

with Anubhab Gupta, William McWilliams, and Angana Chatterjee

Analyzing how market concentration in the meat packing industry affects both agricultural producers and consumers through changes in pricing and market structure.

Trust in Hypothetical Blockchain Claims

with William Secor and Benjamin Campbell

Investigating consumer trust and perceptions of blockchain technology claims in agricultural supply chains and food systems.

Teaching Philosophy

I believe in creating an engaging learning environment that bridges theory and practice. My approach emphasizes hands-on application of econometric methods to real-world agricultural and trade policy questions, helping students develop both technical skills and critical thinking abilities.
